# Diane Wiredu
Diane Wiredu is a messaging strategist and founder of Lion Words, specializing in helping B2B SaaS companies clarify and sharpen their positioning.

Her work focuses on:
- Message-market fit
- Differentiation
- Buyer psychology
- Converting unclear value into compelling narratives

Unlike typical content strategists, Diane doesn’t start with content.

She starts with:
> “Do people actually understand what you do — and why it matters?”

---

## Core Insight: Content Fails When Messaging Is Weak
One of her strongest positions:
> Most B2B companies don’t have a copy problem.  
> They have a **messaging problem**.

Common pattern:
- Teams rewrite their website multiple times
- Invest in content
- Improve copy

But still:
- Prospects don’t “get it”
- Conversion stays low
- Differentiation is unclear

### Implication for LinkedIn
If your messaging is unclear:
- No post will perform consistently
- No content strategy will scale

Because:
> Content amplifies clarity — or confusion

## Message-Market Fit (Before Content-Market Fit)
Diane introduces a critical layer often missed:
Before content works, messaging must land.
Her 3-part framework:
### 1️⃣ Find Your Focus
- Stop trying to say everything
- Choose ONE core message
- Make everything else support it
Signal of weak messaging:
- Too many ideas
- Too many “ands”
- Too many adjectives

---

### 2️⃣ Translate the Value
Most B2B messaging explains:
- What the product does
But fails to show:
- What the user can actually achieve

Her structure:
Feature → What it does → Why it matters → What this enables

---

### 3️⃣ Make It Meaningful
Strong messaging must:
- Be specific
- Be differentiated
- Be rooted in customer language

Test:
> If your competitor can say the same thing → rewrite it

---

## Jargon vs Buzzwords (A Critical Distinction)
One of her most nuanced insights:
> Jargon is not the enemy. Buzzwords are.

### Jargon:
- Insider language
- Signals understanding
- Builds credibility with the right audience

### Buzzwords:
- Vague
- Overused
- Emotionally appealing but empty

Examples:
- “Seamless”
- “Supercharge”
- “Next-gen solution”

---

### Implication for LinkedIn
Good content:
- Sounds like your audience
Bad content:
- Sounds like marketing

---

## Differentiation: The Missing Layer
A major issue she highlights:
> Most companies *have* differentiation — but it doesn’t show up in their messaging.

Common mistakes:
- Trying to win on too many things
- Relying on generic claims (“reliable”, “industry leader”)
- Focusing on post-purchase benefits instead of buying triggers
- Not framing differences against alternatives

---

### Key Insight:
> A differentiator only matters in context.
Without comparison:
- It’s just a claim
With context:
- It becomes a reason to choose

---

## Messaging → Content Alignment
One of her strongest implicit frameworks:
If messaging is unclear:
- Content becomes inconsistent
- Teams are misaligned
- Growth stalls
If messaging is clear:
- Content becomes scalable
- Narrative becomes consistent
- Teams align naturally

---

## Objection Handling (Underrated in B2B Content)
From her Spotify example:
> The best messaging acknowledges friction — not hides it.

Instead of:
- Ignoring objections
- Over-explaining

Do:
- Acknowledge reality
- Speak like a human
- Build trust through honesty

---

### LinkedIn Application
Strong posts:
- Call out real frustrations
- Address objections directly
- Mirror internal buyer thoughts

---

## Messaging as a System (Not Just Copy)
Her work shows that messaging should:
- Guide homepage copy
- Align internal teams
- Shape sales conversations
- Drive all marketing output

---

### Key Shift:
From:
- Writing better content
To:
- Building a **clear narrative system**

---

## What This Means for LinkedIn (B2B SaaS)
Applying Diane Wiredu’s thinking:
You stop asking:
> “What should we post?”

And start asking:
> “Is our message clear enough to scale through content?”

---

### Practical Shift
Instead of:
- Posting frequently with different ideas

Do:
1. Define your core message  
2. Clarify your differentiation  
3. Translate value into outcomes  
4. Use LinkedIn to reinforce the same narrative  

---

## Key Takeaways
- Messaging clarity > content volume  
- One strong message beats many weak ones  
- Differentiation must be visible, not internal  
- Jargon builds connection (when used correctly)  
- Buzzwords destroy clarity  
- Content only works when messaging is already strong  

---

## Positioning Summary
| Weak Approach | Strong Approach |
|------|--------|
| Fixing copy repeatedly | Fixing messaging first |
| Saying everything | Saying one clear thing |
| Generic claims | Contextual differentiation |
| Avoiding objections | Addressing them directly |
| Buzzword-heavy content | Customer-language content |
