## Discovery Call Opening Mistakes
Link:
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/samsalesli_samsales-perfectdiscoverycall-activity-7442203330816196608-ynKx

### Key Insights

- Narrow questions kill conversations
  → Example: “What interested you in taking this call?”  
  → Leads to short, surface-level answers

- Poor questioning leads to poor outcomes
  → Sellers default to demo too early  
  → Or ask random follow-ups without direction

- Discovery calls depend on early framing
  → First minutes define the entire conversation trajectory  
  → Hard to recover once misaligned

- Rapport building must be intentional
  → Based on prior research (Show Me You Know Me)  
  → Not generic small talk

## Executive Content ROI on LinkedIn
Link:
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/samsalesli_samsales-lieb-activity-7450162631950241792-po3C

### Key Insights

- Executive content significantly outperforms company content
  → ~600% more reach

- Trust is built through human voices
  → People trust individuals ~90% more than brands

- User-generated content > polished corporate content
  → Authenticity drives engagement and credibility

- Thought leadership directly impacts revenue
  → 87% of executives made purchase decisions after consuming it

- LinkedIn content influences multiple business functions
  → Sales, recruiting, brand, media, internal communication

- Competitive advantage is time-sensitive
  → Early adopters gain disproportionate benefits  
  → Advantage shrinks as adoption increases

## Article: Coaching Discovery Calls — Part I
Link:
https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/coaching-discovery-calls-what-good-looks-like-part-i-mckenna-b4mpe/

### Key Insights

- Discovery calls are under-coached but critical
  → High impact on sales outcomes  
  → Often avoided due to lack of time or knowledge

- First 15 minutes determine success
  → Requires detailed coaching and analysis

- Rapport building must be differentiated
  → Avoid generic small talk  
  → Use pre-call research to stand out

- Broad, open-ended questions drive depth
  → Encourage buyers to share context and problems  
  → Prevent shallow conversations

- Executive presence matters
  → Engagement, attention, and tone affect perception

## Article: Coaching Discovery Calls — Part II
Link:
https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/coaching-discovery-calls-what-good-looks-like-part-ii-mckenna-ffpye/

### Key Insights

- Active listening is the #1 buyer expectation
  → Includes verbal and non-verbal signals

- Engagement signals build trust
  → Reactions, acknowledgment, note-taking

- No interruption → better information flow
  → Allows full context from buyer

- Structured responses improve clarity
  → Summarize → then explore deeper

- Coaching should be consistent and repeatable
  → Build habits (1:1 + team coaching cadence)
