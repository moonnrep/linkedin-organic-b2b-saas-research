# LinkedIn Organic Content Playbook (B2B SaaS)

## Objective
Build a repeatable system where LinkedIn content:
- creates trust at scale
- shapes buyer perception before sales
- converts attention into pipeline

This is not a content calendar.
This is a **demand generation system using content**.

---

## 1. Positioning (Foundation Layer)
### Rule

Define one clear audience + one clear problem.

If positioning is unclear, content will not compound.

**Why this matters:**
- Content amplifies clarity — or confusion
- Broad messaging leads to weak engagement and low conversion

**Execution:**
- Identify ICP (role, company stage, problem context)
- Define one core problem to own
- Build all content around that problem

(Source: Diane Wiredu, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/dianewiredu_saastock2025-activity-7383477148470222848-QuFN, LinkedIn post)

---

## Practical Standard
If a competitor can say the same thing → positioning is weak.

---

## 2. Content Strategy (Authority Layer)
**Rule**

Authority content > frequent content

High-performing content is:
- opinionated
- specific
- experience-based

Not:
- generic tips
- recycled frameworks

**Execution:**

Each post must include at least one:
- real observation
- contrarian insight
- operational breakdown

(Source: Kevin Dorsey, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kddorsey3_adobeacrobatpartner-activity-7448396119035805696-b4a-?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAD0RJCEB8WP-QLGcqKLluo9Ci1_-4zr0S2c, LinkedIn post)

---

## Content Structure (repeatable format)

1. Hook → tension or contradiction
2. Insight → reframe
3. Explanation → mechanism
4. Action → minimal, specific

---

## 3. Content Creation (Asset Thinking)

**Rule**

Create fewer, higher-leverage assets

Instead of:
- daily random post

Focus on:
- 1 strong idea → multiple outputs

**Execution:**

Turn one core idea into:
- insight post
- story post
- educational breakdown
- carousel

(Source: Erin Balsa, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/erinbalsa_marketing-teams-want-to-do-something-more-activity-7442913184354750464-sh_H?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAD0RJCEB8WP-QLGcqKLluo9Ci1_-4zr0S2c, LinkedIn post)

---

## 4. Distribution (Core Growth Lever)
**Rule**

Content that is not distributed does not exist.

Most teams:
> create → post → move on

High-performing teams:
> create → distribute → re-distribute → optimize

**Execution:**
- repost strong ideas with new angles
- repurpose into multiple formats
- engage in comments to extend reach

(Source: Ross Simmonds, https://rosssimmonds.com/blog/visibility-llms/, 27.03.2026)

---

## Distribution Standard
One idea should appear multiple time over time.

---

## 5. Personal Brand Layer
**Rule**

People outperform companies on LinkedIn.

Buyers trust:
- individuals
- experiences
- perspectives

Not:
- polished brand messaging

**Execution:**
- prioritize founder/operator voice
- write in first-hand perspective
- avoid corporate tone

(Source: Samantha McKenna, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/samsalesli_samsales-lieb-activity-7450162631950241792-po3C?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAD0RJCEB8WP-QLGcqKLluo9Ci1_-4zr0S2c, LinkedIn post)

---

## 6. Demand Capture (Conversion Layer)
**Rule**

Engagement signals intent.

Not all attention is equal.

**High-value signals:**
- comments
- repeat engagement
- topic-specific interaction

**Execution:**
- track commenters
- identify recurring names
- engage directly

(Source: Suprava Sabat, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/priyankas111_how-to-steal-your-competitors-leads-activity-7426920600331505664-goLk?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAD0RJCEB8WP-QLGcqKLluo9Ci1_-4zr0S2c, LinkedIn post)

---

## 7. Content as Pre-Sales
**Rule**

Content shapes the buyer before sales conversations begin.

Buyers:
- learn before talking
- form opinions early

**Execution:**

Content should:
- define the problem
- shape the solution
- position your approach

(Source: Samantha McKenna, https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/coaching-discovery-calls-what-good-looks-like-part-i-mckenna-b4mpe/, 07.04.2025)

---

## 8. Weekly Execution Loop (Operational System)
This system runs in cycles:
1. Define one core idea (based on ICP problem)
2. Create one high-signal post
3. Repurpose into 2–3 variations
4. Distribute across the week
5. Track engagement (comments, repeat names)
6. Extract insights from responses
7. Feed into next content cycle

(Source: Synthesized from Ross Simmonds, Erin Balsa, Suprava Sabat)

## Where Expert Disagree

### 1. Content Format (Text vs Multi-format)

**Kevin Dorsey:**
- text-only works
- idea density > visuals

**Ross Simmonds:**
- multi-format distribution is critical
- repurposing increases reach

**My take:**
Multi-format is more scalable in early stages because it increases distribution surface area.

However, text-first content becomes more effective in niche, high-trust audiences where depth matters more than reach.

Decision:
Start multi-format → transition to depth as audience matures.

---

### 2. Content Source (Data vs Experience)

**Caitlin Lemon:**
- data should drive content decisions
- focus on real behavioral signals

(Source: Caitlin Lemon, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/caitlinlemon_insightsanalyst-strategy-aistrategy-activity-7450894588489285632-k9Fc?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAD0RJCEB8WP-QLGcqKLluo9Ci1_-4zr0S2c, 20.04.2026)

**Kevin Dorsey:**
- experience and reframing build authority

**My take:**
Both are are required, but they serve different roles.
- Data identifies what matters
- Experience explains why it matters

Decision:
Use data to guide topics, experience to deliver insights. 

---

### 3. Distribution Focus (Linkedin vs Multi-channel)
**Ross Simmonds:**
- multi-channel is required

**Erin Balsa:**
- Linkedin can be central distribution hub (Source: Erin Balsa, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/erinbalsa_if-i-were-leading-marketing-at-a-b2b-saas-activity-7442194208964354048-bq8S?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAD0RJCEB8WP-QLGcqKLluo9Ci1_-4zr0S2c, LinkedIn post)

**My take:**
Multi-channel is the long-term advantage, but not the starting point.

Reason: 
- easier execution
- faster feedback loop

Decision:
Start with LinkedIn for speed and feedback → expand to multi-channel once messaging is validated.

## What I Rejected and Why
### 1. “Post More Content to Grow Faster”

Rejected because:
- increases volume without improving quality
- leads to generic, low-signal content

Better approach:
- fewer, higher-quality ideas

---

### 2. “Use AI to Generate Content at Scale”

Rejected because:
- produces generic output
- weakens differentiation

(Source: Caitlin Lemon — tool-first thinking problem
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/caitlinlemon_promptengineering-strategy-businessstrategy-activity-7449795581197049857-dh-E, LinkedIn post)

Decision:
Use AI for support (repurposing, structuring), not thinking.

### 3. "Ungated content should always be the default"
(Source: Erin Balsa — ungated content strategy
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/erinbalsa_choosing-not-to-gate-your-report-is-a-bold-activity-7448329865499611136-jqyp)

Rejected as a default strategy.

Reason:
- reduces attribution clarity
- harder to connect content to pipeline in early-stage teams

Decision:
Use ungated content selectively, not universally.
