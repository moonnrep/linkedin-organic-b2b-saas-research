# Ross Simmonds
This section is based on analysis of two of Ross Simmonds’ YouTube videos:
- Create Once, Distribute Forever: How Great Creators Spread Ideas and How You Can too
 > https://youtu.be/w1-bmDmbjIo?si=HhYaixAXuPXRh6DX

- The Growth Framework That Leads Marketing Teams To Earn & Reach Millions
 > https://youtu.be/6UzikJ9XCr8?si=ENka6l7NmeT6AxH7  

Ross Simmonds is widely known for his focus on **content distribution** — a critical but often overlooked part of modern content marketing.

---

## Core Insight
One of the biggest mindset shifts he emphasizes across both videos:
> Content marketing is not just about creating content.  
> It’s about making sure that content actually gets seen.

For years, marketers have been told:
> “Just create more content.”

But in reality, that advice is incomplete.
Creating content is only half the job.  
The other half — and often the more important one — is **distribution**.

---

## Create Once, Distribute Forever
One of Ross’ core philosophies:
> If your content is good, it shouldn’t be used once.

Instead of constantly creating new content from scratch,  
you should focus on **maximizing the value of what you’ve already created**.

This includes:
- Turning 1 idea into multiple formats  
- Sharing it across different channels  
- Reposting it with new angles  
- Bringing it back over time  

Good content compounds — but only if you keep distributing it.

---

## The Content Growth Framework
Ross introduces a simple but powerful system.
Most teams are stuck here:
- Create → Publish → Move on  

They believe that once content is live, the job is done.
But high-performing teams operate differently.

They follow a full-cycle system:
- Research → Create → Distribute → Optimize  

---

### Research (Where Strategy Starts)
Before creating anything, they ask:
- Who is the audience?
- What problems are they trying to solve?
- What content are they already engaging with?

They analyze:
- Communities (Reddit, forums, comments)
- Competitor content
- Media consumption habits

This leads to:
> **Content-Market Fit** — content that naturally resonates with the audience.

---

### Create (With Intent)
They don’t create randomly.

Every piece of content is designed to:
- Educate  
- Engage  
- Entertain  
- Empower  

If it doesn’t do at least one of these, it’s not worth publishing.

---

### Distribute (The Real Differentiator)
This is where most teams fail — and where top teams win.

They don’t rely on:
- One post  
- One channel  
- One moment  

They:
- Repurpose content into multiple formats  
- Share it repeatedly with different hooks  
- Place it where their audience already is  

Distribution is not an afterthought.  
It is part of the strategy from the beginning.

---

### Optimize (The Compounding Effect)
They don’t forget content after publishing.

Instead, they:
- Revisit old content  
- Improve structure and messaging  
- Update insights and data  
- Re-distribute high-performing content  

This is how content becomes a **long-term asset**, not a one-time effort.

---

## Observed Pattern from Ross Simmonds’ Content
Across both videos, a clear pattern emerges:

- Most marketers over-invest in content creation  
- Very few invest enough in distribution  
- High-performing teams treat distribution as a **system**, not an action  

This gap is exactly where most strategies fail.

---

## What This Means for LinkedIn (B2B SaaS)
While Ross discusses multi-channel distribution, this project applies his ideas specifically to **LinkedIn** — the primary organic channel for B2B SaaS.

LinkedIn is not just a place to post.  
It is a **distribution engine for B2B attention**.

In B2B SaaS:
- Buyers are active on LinkedIn  
- Decision-makers consume content daily  
- Trust is built before any sales conversation happens  

---

### The Strategic Shift
You stop asking:
> “What should I post today?”
And start asking:
> “How can I distribute this idea multiple times to reach the right audience?”

---

### LinkedIn Execution Layer
Instead of creating new content every day:
Turn 1 core idea into multiple LinkedIn formats:
- Story post → relatable experience  
- Insight post → industry POV  
- Educational post → step-by-step breakdown  
- Carousel → visual framework  
- Comments → expand reach under relevant posts  

Same idea. Multiple entry points.

---

### Why This Works for B2B SaaS
In SaaS, people don’t convert instantly.

They:
- See your content  
- See it again  
- Recognize your thinking  
- Start trusting your expertise  

Distribution creates **familiarity**  
Familiarity builds **trust**  
Trust drives **pipeline**

---

### The Hidden Advantage
Most competitors:
- Post once  
- Chase new ideas  
- Ignore distribution  

If you:
- Repurpose consistently  
- Repost strategically  
- Stay visible over time  

You win — not because you create more,  
but because you **stay top of mind longer**.

---

## Final Takeaway
- Content = asset  
- Distribution = leverage  
- Consistency = unfair advantage
